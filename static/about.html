About — Northbrook Observatory

About

Northbrook Observatory.

A 501(c)(3) public-research observatory on Ridge Road, eight miles west of the village of Northbrook. We host monthly public observing nights, school programs from October through May, and a small research portfolio centered on photometry of variable stars.

Mission.

The observatory exists for two reasons: to give people who don't otherwise get to look through a forty-inch telescope a chance to do so, and to keep the building standing through another generation. Every program we run, every grant we accept, every dollar we raise points back at one of those two ends.

Timeline.

From the founding to the current campaign

1888
Edgar Northbrook builds a 12-inch refractor on his family's ridge property. The dome is wood-framed, hand-cranked, and clad in copper.Founding
1923
Edgar's estate donates the observatory and 14 acres of surrounding forest to the town of Northbrook on the condition that it remain free to the public.Donation
1953
NSF Cooperative grant funds replacement of the original optics with a 40-inch f/8 Cassegrain reflector. The dome is electrified the same year.Modernization
1978
Dome rotation motor replaced after a winter ice-storm jammed the original. Volunteer machine shop in Plymouth fabricates the gear ring.Mechanical refit
1991
Observation floor rebuilt in laminated oak. Original 1888 floorboards moved to a display in the lobby.Floor refit
2018
Capital campaign funds full mount + control-system rebuild — Paramount ME II head, closed-loop encoders, CCD imager, low-resolution spectrograph.Drive refit
2024
Roof copper recladding completed. Original 1888 copper retained where intact; new copper sourced from a Vermont mill.Envelope refit

Retired ham-radio operator from Lincoln, NH. Runs the Friday night dome rotation. Knows every constellation by feel and can find M13 in fog.

MS Physics, UNH 2021. Maintains the imaging pipeline and the website. Splits time between the observatory and her family's farm in Bath, two valleys north.
Board of trustees
Seven trustees, three-year staggered terms. Two seats reserved for Northbrook town residents; one for a representative of the Abenaki Heritage Trust. Currently chaired by Dr. Jane Ouellette (UNH Physics, emeritus).

Support.

We are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. The town of Northbrook covers the land tax and one-third of utilities; the rest comes from program fees, grants, and individual giving. Roughly 40% of our annual budget arrives in October and November as year-end donations — if you've been to a public night and enjoyed it, that's when the gift goes furthest.

Land acknowledgement.

The observatory and the ridge it stands on are within the unceded ancestral territory of the Cowasuck Band of the Pennacook-Abenaki People. We host an annual stargazing evening in collaboration with the Abenaki Heritage Trust on the spring equinox, and 5% of public-night program fees are forwarded to the Trust's land-rematriation program.
