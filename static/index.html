Common questions

Is this open source?

Yes. Forge, Loom, Crawler, Annotator, CMS, LFI all ship under MIT OR Apache-2.0.

Every commit is signed; every release ships a CycloneDX SBOM with SLSA-style attestation.

How does the AI integration work?

The Critic trait is the seam. Every AI proposal flows through Critic::evaluate → Decision. Raw LLM output cannot reach commit() — it's a type error, not a code-review policy.

Bring your own LLM provider; the LFI-backed Critic is opt-in via Cargo feature.

Can I host on Tor or IPFS?

Yes. Forge ships typed DeployAdapter implementations for clearnet, Tor onion, I2P eepsite, IPFS/IPNS, Gemini, and Lokinet. Your audience picks the network.

Do you train models on my content?

No. We don't see your content at all. PlausiDen is self-host-first; you build the artifact locally and we have no access to it.

If you use the LFI integration with a third-party LLM provider, that provider's policy applies; bring your own key, route directly, no PlausiDen-side proxy.

What does pricing look like?

Free for solo. $0 for the first three team seats. After that, by build-minute, not by user.

Enterprise is custom with SLA + same-day support + SOC 2 II artifacts.

Is content portable out?

Your content is JSON in git. Walk away with a tar.gz. The platform produces no proprietary artifact.
