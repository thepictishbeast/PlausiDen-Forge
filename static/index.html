Northbrook Observatory — Public astronomy in the Northern Highlands

Northbrook Observatory · Grafton County, NH

A small observatory under a darker sky.

We point a forty-inch Cassegrain reflector at the night sky on the second Friday of every month, and we tell anyone who shows up what we're looking at. Founded 1888. Still here.

What we are.

A public-research observatory on a wooded ridge 1,840 feet above sea level. The dome holds a forty-inch f/8 Cassegrain reflector built in 1953 and refurbished in 2018. The site is dark enough to read Bortle 3 most clear nights and Bortle 2 in winter, when humidity drops and the lake-effect cloud cover thins.

Aperture
40 inches (1016 mm). Cassegrain, f/8 system. Two instrument ports for a CCD imager and a low-resolution spectrograph.
Drive
German equatorial mount, refurbished 2018 with a Software Bisque Paramount ME II head and a closed-loop encoder. Tracks at sidereal rate to better than 1 arcsecond over a 20-minute exposure.
Site
Bortle 3 typical, Bortle 2 in dry winter conditions. Mean seeing 2.4 arcseconds FWHM. Mean transparency 0.86 mag/airmass.
Staff
One full-time director, two part-time observers, a board of seven, and a rotating cast of volunteer hosts on public nights.

What's coming up.

We publish a monthly sky report on the first of every month. It's a one-page PDF: where to look, when to look, what's worth waiting up for, what's worth getting up early for. Subscribe below and we'll mail it to you. We will not sell your address; we will not mail you anything else.

How we got here.

Edgar Northbrook built the original 12-inch refractor on this ridge in 1888 because the air was clear and the railroad ran near enough that the equipment could be hauled by team. He donated the observatory to the town of Northbrook in 1923. The 40-inch Cassegrain replaced the refractor in 1953 with NSF support. The building was electrified the same year. The dome motor was replaced in 1978, the floor in 1991, and the entire mount and control system in 2018. Most of the brass is original.
